What is TMS?

A Breakthrough in Depression, PTSD, Anxiety & OCD Treatment

Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation (TMS) is an FDA-cleared, non-invasive, drug-free treatment that uses focused magnetic pulses to stimulate underactive nerve cells in the prefrontal cortex — the brain region governing mood. It's proven effective for Major Depressive Disorder (MDD), Treatment-Resistant Depression (TRD), OCD, and PTSD.

When antidepressants like SSRIs, SNRIs, or tricyclics fail to provide adequate relief after two or more trials, your condition may qualify as Treatment-Resistant Depression (TRD) — the primary indication TRICARE covers for TMS therapy.

The Science

How TMS Therapy Works

It's not you — it's the brain. Depression often stems from underactive neural circuits. TMS uses precisely targeted magnetic pulses to restore healthy brain activity — no drugs, no surgery, no downtime.

01
🧲

Magnetic Pulse Generation

A specialized coil placed near the scalp generates focused magnetic pulses — similar in strength to an MRI — targeting the left dorsolateral prefrontal cortex.

02
⚡

Neural Stimulation

The magnetic pulses painlessly stimulate underactive neurons in brain regions responsible for mood regulation, working memory, and emotional processing.

03
🔗

Neuroplasticity Changes

Over multiple sessions, the brain develops new, healthier neural pathways — rewiring circuits that medication alone couldn't reach. This is lasting, structural change.

04
🌟

Lasting Improvement

Most patients experience sustained relief even after treatment ends. Results continue to build as the brain maintains its new, healthier patterns of activity.
